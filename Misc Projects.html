"Cap’n Moby"

Though a smaller project I thought I had a lot of fun with this and wanted to showcase it. Given a rigged and animated character, I was tasked with implementing said animations in a game setting, and I decided to throw in a little extra flair. I created a third person camera setup, as well as a weighted constraint based multi-aim rig to have the character look wherever the camera was looking. In the process of messing with rig settings, I discovered some fun interactions that allowed me to cause the character to ragdoll, and finally, I added some controls to fly around while ragdolled.

"Bunker Hill" and "Treehouse Meathouse"

Bunker Hill was a solo project, and Treehouse was one third of a group project where we learned about level streaming. Both were completed using the same shooter asset pack and a lot of custom modeled Unreal geometry. Bunker Hill I based off my experience playing Overwatch with a similar layout to Ilios Lighthouse, the bunker itself being a point to capture and the trenches a combat zone between it and the spawns. With the base layout down a lot of work went into making the various sightlines and spaces more interesting with decorative boulders and traps, some boxes for climbing out of the trenches, and of course the destroyed tank in the center, of which the barrels can be climbed through as a flanking entrance to the bunker. Finally I played around with Unreal’s physics settings to make pushable cover pillars inside the bunker on a sort of magnet grid. Treehouse has a much simpler linear layout, but took a lot more modeling time, the terrain I built for the level's background uses a custom 3 layer material, and the terrain sculpt, treehouses, trees, and furniture are also all custom made. Both include shortcuts, objectives, and health/ammo packs to incentivize out of the way sections, and full accessibility to the bot players.
